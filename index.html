Course Description

This course will teach you the fundamentals of data analytics in a business context. You will learn how to collect, process, analyze, and visualize data to make informed business decisions.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the role of data analytics in business
  • Learn various data collection and processing techniques
  • Apply statistical methods to analyze data
  • Create informative data visualizations
  • Develop data-driven decision-making skills

Course Outline

  1. Introduction to Business Data Analytics
  2. Data Collection and Processing
  3. Exploratory Data Analysis
  4. Statistical Methods for Data Analysis
  5. Data Visualization Techniques
  6. Data-Driven Decision Making
  7. Case Studies and Projects

Léonard Boussioux

Léonard Boussioux is an assistant professor in information systems and operations management at the University of Washington, Foster School of Business, with an adjunct position at the Allen School of Computer Science & Engineering. He holds a PhD in Operations Research from MIT. He is working on analytics for healthcare, climate, sustainability, and a better world, and AI for design and biomimicry with Oxman. He is also affiliated with Harvard's Digital Data Design Institute, where he researches AI for the Future of Work and Innovation.

His research and entrepreneurial work have received wide recognition, including giving two TEDx talks, winning the MIT 3-minute thesis competition, and awards from INFORMS, MIT, IEEE, UNESCO, and Google. He previously interned at Google X, Mila, UC Berkeley, and the French National Centre for Scientific Research. Léonard is also a passionate teacher and has received multiple teaching awards at MIT, including the Goodwin Medal, the institute's highest teaching award for a student. Léonard launched a new educational effort, the Universal Artificial Intelligence Institute, to teach the latest technological advancements to vast audiences.
